Alex Thorson
Head Coach

Head Coach Alex Thorson is in his first year as head coach of the Cavaliers. He comes to KCC after serving two years as a graduate assistant men’s basketball coach at Olivet Nazarene University. Coach Thorson brings a great passion and tremendous knowledge for the game to the Cavaliers.

At Olivet, Thorson was mentored by Illinois Basketball Coaches Association, Chicagoland Collegiate Athletic Conference Athletics, and National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics Hall of Fame Coach Ralph Hodge who is entering his 40th season as head coach of the Tigers.

Coach Thorson brings experience in recruiting, team preparation, player development, strength and conditioning, and building relationships. With his connections in the Chicagoland, Central Illinois, and Indiana areas, he is excited to start building a winning environment for the KCC Men’s Basketball Team.

A native of Philo, Ill., he attended the High School of St. Thomas More in Champaign for three years, and completed his high school career at his hometown school, Tolono Unity. He accepted a scholarship to continue his college career at Olivet, and played point guard for four seasons and 101 games. He is 19th all-time in assists for the Tigers. Thorson led the CCAC in assists (157), assists per game (5.41), and assist/turnover ratio (3.6:1) his senior season. In his final two seasons, the Tigers played in the NAIA Div. II National Championship; including a junior campaign where the team won the CCAC Tournament title and Regular Season championship.

Thorson graduated from Olivet in 2016 with a bachelor's degree in sports management. He is completing a Master's of Business Administration, also from Olivet.

Coach Thorson is excited to build a championship culture in the men’s basketball program at KCC. He looks forward to encouraging players every day to better themselves from an academic, social, and basketball standpoint.

Matt Neaville
Assistant Coach

Coach Matt Neaville is in his first year as Assistant Men’s Basketball Coach at Kankakee Community College.  He brings experience in many different areas which include; recruiting, team preparation, player development, and building relationships with players and coaches.  Matt Neaville is from Urbana, Illinois and he prepped at Urbana High School where he was named All-Area first team, All-State, and MVP as a junior and senior. Matt Neaville played collegiately at Parkland Community College where he was named a third team All-American performer. In 2006-07, Neaville averaged 26.2 points per game, which ranked him fourth in the nation in scoring. Neaville set a new junior college national record for three pointers in a game with 17. Neaville played for head coach Chris Warren at Parkland and was named conference Player of the Year, Freshman Player of the Year, All-Conference, and All-Region. Neaville went on to play at Union University in Jackson, Tennessee. 

Neaville was named a first team performer for his play during the 2008-09 season. In his two seasons as a Bulldog, Neaville reached the 1,000 point mark for his career in just 44 games and tallied 1,375 career points. He stands in second place for made three pointers in a career at Union with 290 three pointers. He also holds the record for three pointers in a season (153) and three pointers made in a game (11) at Union. Neaville was named an NAIA first team All-American and TranSouth Conference Player of the Year.  He graduated with his Bachelors in Sports Management and Marketing. 

Coach Neaville went on to play professional basketball in Mexico once he graduated from Union University.  He averaged 17 ppg in his first season in professional basketball. After he played in Mexico, Coach Neaville played semi-professional basketball throughout the states.  He played for Springfield Xpress, Kankakee Soldiers, Danville Riverhawks and Champaign Swarm. He retired from professional basketball in 2014.  In 2015, Coach Neaville was Director of Basketball Operations and Assistant Men’s Basketball Coach for the Champaign Swarm.  During the 2015 season, he ended up taking over Head Coaching duties for Coach John Spezia as he stepped down.  In 2016-2018, Coach Neaville was the Assistant Men’s Basketball Coach for the Danville Coal Miners.  Danville Coal Miners played in the CBA, semiprofessional basketball league.  Coach Neaville also coached for St. Thomas School in Philo Illinois.  He served as the Head Coach for the 8th grade boys’ basketball team from 2012-2015.  Coach Neaville also owns his own basketball academy named, Neaville Basketball Academy.  He gives training and lessons to the youth and young adults.  He plans on keeping it in Urbana, Illinois and also bringing it to the Kankakee area.  Coach Neaville has one son, Jaden Neaville that is 7 years old.
