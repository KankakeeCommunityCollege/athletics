 Season Preview

The Lady Cavaliers Women's Basketball Team begins their season on Saturday, November 2nd at 1:00pm as they take on Spoon River College at home.

Casey Meister returns as Head Coach for her fourth season and is extremely excited for the new season. Her excitement and high expectations come from the work she put in recruiting to the rich history of Cavalier basketball that once held a standard of 20+ wins per season.

Meister's 2023-2024 group finished with 14 wins, short of their team goal. Even after that, Meister continues to take on a challenging schedule playing nationally ranked teams in pursuit of becoming the best team possible for the postseason.

The 24'-25 Cavalier roster features plenty of new faces. In total, there are 13 freshmen coming in. Meister gets straight to the point by stating “The Freshman are going to have to make an impact for us right away.” Among them are six players from the Peoria area; Adriana Barber, Tania Cox, Ava Simpson, Alana Ross, Alyssa Ross and Janessa Wallace. Three athletes from Indiana; Malaysia Flanagan, Kayla Lacombe and Johnetta Whitmire. Khiara Cerdeira from Florida, Mariya Babilius from Wisconsin and two locals; Lynlee Jubin and Lexi Salazar. Meister is hoping that the familiarity among the players helps build team unity and camaraderie quickly as she knows, this is a huge part of success.

The Lady Cavs have two sophomores returning. Among those two sophomores, Jaida Taylor, will take a medical redshirt due to a torn ACL and will not play this season. Jaida will remain very involved and has been given the title of team captain. Harmony Reynolds is the only seasoned sophomore returning to the floor. Reynolds is able to share what it takes to grind through a full college season and looks to play a pivotal role for the team as a leader.

Coach Meister spoke honestly by stating “This year is going to be challenging; we have a tough schedule and a whole new squad. The quicker the players learn, the more successful our season will be. It's going to take some time to figure out how to be a college athlete,” She followed up with “Once they figure that out, I think we'll be okay. I'm confident because this group competes and already has an edge about them.”

The expectations for this season are high for the Lady Cavaliers. Coach Meister expects this team to be fast and put a lot of points on the board and hopes that leads to team wins.
