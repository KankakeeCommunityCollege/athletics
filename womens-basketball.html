 Season Preview

The Kankakee Lady Cavaliers will step on the court looking a little different this year as they will have a new Head Coach for the first time in 26 years. Casey Meister is taking over the position with a notoriously successful history behind her and a bright future ahead of her. With multiple Region IV Championships and National Tournament success to build on, Coach Meister looks to use that history to her advantage. 

The Lady Cavaliers look to turn the page and move on from the last two seasons that were riddled with changes, quarantines, and cancelations due to Covid-19. The team experienced the effects of Covid-19 firsthand in 2020 as they finished winning their 23rd Region Title as the No. 3 seed. Just days before the National Tournament, the nation shut down sports causing them to finish 14th in the country by default. In 2021 the pandemic struck again a day before their Region IV Tournament was set to begin. They had no choice but to shut down the season for good and walk away before getting a chance to fight for another Region Championship.

 

This experience, along with new energy on campus, has fueled the returning players to work even harder towards a postseason experience. The new freshman will have a front row seat to the effort, hustle, and dedication it takes to be successful at the collegiate level.

 

After assisting the Lady Cavs for the last seven seasons and being a part of so much success, Meister plans on keeping the foundations of which this program was built while putting her own stamp on the season.

 

Success this season will depend on the growth, leadership, and the ability of the freshman to learn quickly and produce on the court equally fast. The five freshmen looking to make their mark this season include: guard Danielle Lille, guard Summer Hill, guard Emma Robles, and guard/forward Amarii Mays. 

 

The eight returning sophomores will be expected to lead by example and continue the hard-nosed style of play that has produced great success for the Lady Cavs. The goal for 2021-2022 Lady Cavaliers is to bring home the program's 24th Region Title back to where it belongs.
