 Season Preview

The Cavaliers softball program look to make their mark on the 2020 season with 10 sophomores and 8 freshman players. With a wide variety of pitching, hitting, and defense, they expect to be a strong contender for their 22st consecutive NJCAA D-2 Region Championship, and make another trip to the National Tournament in Clinton, MS.

The Cavaliers will depend on the strong play and leadership of the 8 returning sophomore starters from last year’s squad that finished 13th in the country. Matti Lanie led the region in stolen bases in 2019 and  will once again patrol Centerfield and lead fellow speedsters Madalin Evans (So - Shortstop), Jenna Raines (Fr - Outfield), Makenna Wessels (Fr - Outfield), and McKenna Goldtrap (Fr - Outfield).  Gabby Schultz (So -3B) finished 5th in home runs and 2nd in runs batted in in the region and will continue to provide consistent hitting and power in front other power bats Ashley Riner (So – 3B), Emma Schriner (So – Outfield), Aubrey Durdan (So – P/1B). Kenna Kleinert (Fr – 2B) looks to bring timely hitting and great defense and leadership to fellow incoming freshman Kayden Mines (Fr – 1B), and Alexis Britton (Fr – C). Payton Kerness (So – C) will be expected to help lead the team and anchor the catchers as the only returning catcher from last year’s championship run. The Cavs solid pitching will be expected to dominate with returning starters Maddie Wallace (So – P) who finished 3rd in earned run average and 5th in strikeouts in the region, and Hayley Edmonds (So – P), with the addition of Shelbi Westfall (Fr – P).  The incoming transfers of Lanie Malone (Fr – MI) and Paige Glass (So – C) will provide more depth and power to an already potent lineup.

With dependable hitting, power, and speed as well as some veteran top pitching, the Cav’s expect to compete in a tough Region IV with solid opponents Triton, South Suburban, and Prairie State. The schedule also includes match ups against top ranked Rock Valley, Parkland, Danville, Madison, and Lincoln Land, St. Louis With so many top level adversaries, the Cavaliers should be well positioned for a long and successful 2020 season.
