 Season Preview

The Cavaliers softball program look to make their mark on the 2019 season with 2 sophomore and 13 freshman players. With a wide variety of pitching, hitting, and defense, they expect to be a strong contender for their 21st consecutive NJCAA D-2 Region Championship, and make another trip to the National Tournament in Clinton, MS.

The Cavaliers will depend on the strong play and leadership of returning sophomore player Mikayla Cole (P/Uti). Cole will be depended on to provide the knowledge and experience to help guide a young roster. The team roster’s a solid sophomore transfer in Faith Harris (C) who will provide much needed depth and power, that compliments freshman pitchers Maddie Wallace, Hayley Edmonds, Sydney Reams, Aubrey Durdan (P/1B), infielders Gabby Schultz (OF), Amber Mussey, Ashley Riner; catchers Sarah Starr, Leann Mossman, Payton Kerness; and outfielders Madalin Evans (OF/MI), Emme Schriner, and Mattie Lanie.

With dependable hitting, power, and speed as well as some young top pitching, the Cav’s expect to compete in a tough Region IV with solid opponents Triton, South Suburban, and Prairie State. The schedule also includes match ups against top ranked Rock Valley, Parkland, Danville, Madison, and Lincoln Land, as well as multiple top D-1 schools in Florida. With so many top level adversaries, the Cavaliers should be well positioned for a long and successful 2019 season.

Keith Cooper
Head Coach

Head Coach Keith Cooper in his first season with the Cavaliers posted a 27-24 mark on the season while leading his squad to their 20th consecutive NJCAA D-2 Region IV Championship. The well-known Kankakee Community College Cavaliers Softball Program made the trip to the NJCAA D-2 National Tournament in Clinton, MS and finished 12th in the country. Coach Cooper was a community leader in youth sports for 23 years. His involvement includes 23 years coaching various levels of players. He held multiple positions on local league board of directors, as well as President of the Kankakee Knights of Columbus Little League, Knights of Columbus Bronco League, Kankakee Elks Pony League, and the Kankakee Valley Colt and Palomino League.  His involvement in coaching has taken him to coaching many tournaments at Illinois State University in Normal, IL and Central Michigan University in Mt. Pleasant, MI., and has led him to 3 State Fast pitch Softball Tournaments in NSA Softball in Illinois.

                Prior to becoming the head coach of the Lady Cav’s Softball Program, he assisted with the KCC Women’s Basketball team as official scorekeeper for all home games and often traveled with the team.

Cooper was born in Kankakee in 1972. Raised in Limestone, Cooper attended Limestone Grade School. He also attended Kankakee High School class of 1990. Coach Cooper is a former student of Kankakee Community College where he studied Law Enforcement. He worked for AT&T for 18 years.

He and his wife, Lisa, reside in Kankakee. They have 4 children Doug (Traci), Brittany, Jordan, and Austin. They have 3 grandchildren, Autumn, Halle, and Hunter, and will be adding 2 more grandchildren in July 2019.

Kelli Bonomo
Assistant Coach

Kelli is an IBNFC Certified Nutritionist and Personal Trainer. She is the owner of Vault360 Nutrition and fitness studio in Kankakee.

Kelli has been involved in the industry for over 12 years and has spent her time educating clients and athletes on the importance of stabilizing the blood sugar to create balance in the body.

As a strength and conditioning coach as well, she has conditioned MMA fighters and wrestlers as well as bodybuilding and fitness competitors and other athletes. She prides herself on educating each client on the physiological movement of the body to reduce injury, still giving them the ability to push themselves to maximum performance.

Her Nutrition Coaching method is backed by science and physiology rather than diet and deprivation. Feeding the body in a balance to create homeostasis, which also helps keep inflammation lower allowing optimal muscle recovery and repair.

Joe Powers
Assistant Coach

Joseph is married to Misty (Mahoney) from Chebanse, IL. They have three children Sam, Andrew and Emma. 

Joseph has an Associates Degree in Law Enforcement from KCC 94’. Graduated with a Bachelors of Arts Degree from Governors State University 2010 and obtained a Masters in Business Administration from Olivet Nazarene University 2013.

Joseph started coaching right after high school in 1989 at Limestone Little League then began starting coaching softball in 2003. He has coached travel for the TNT Twisters, OAK Stars, River Valley Rage and Midland Magic Organizations. His teams have most notably won USSSA State, 2nd ASA Northern Nationals, ASA Chicago Metro and many other travel weekend tournament championships. Many of his former players have played or are still playing in college.

When ever Joseph has any free time he enjoys jogging and fishing.

John Teders
Assistant Coach

This is John Teders’ first year as Assistant Coach for the Cavaliers.  He joins us with a wealth of sports knowledge and coaching experience.  He grew up in the area and was an all-star athlete throughout his elementary and high school career in football and baseball.  After high school he continued to play sports and played on several slow-pitch softball teams.   John has been involved coaching the past 20-years at youth and high school levels in baseball, softball and football.  He has extensive knowledge of the mechanics of pitching in both girls softball and baseball. 

Outside of sports, John is a union carpenter with many years of experience in residential and commercial construction.  He and his wife, Lori have three children Marissa, Brock & Brenna and a granddaughter, Rylee.
