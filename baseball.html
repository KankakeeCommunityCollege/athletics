 Season Preview

The Cavaliers enter the 2023 season with high expectations. The program has enjoyed recent success, including an NJCAA Division II National Championship in 2017. In 2022, the team finished with a 43-17 record and finished fourth in the Regional 4 Tournament. This spring, the team has plenty of talent, and is poised to make a run at a Region 4 Championship.


The game starts with pitching, and a young and inexperienced staff will be tested early. The returning players and sophomores will be expected to take the lead. The returning pitchers are Nick Barlow, Tanner Sobkoviak, and Trey Bryant; who will welcome transfers Zack Millsap and Will Frisch. Freshman arms will include left-handers Graham Kasey, Jackson Smith and Zack O’Donnell; and righties Ryan Norman, Brett Hecimovich, Aidan Nugent, Drew VanOeveren, Kaleb Waller, Lucuas Frank, Leroy Lepper and Luke Wolff.

“We have competition on the pitching staff and as we continue our spring practices and get into the season, I feel the players will more clearly see their roles.” says Head Coach Todd Post “We have 7-8 potential starting pitchers and a group of arms that will be successful out of the bull pen. We need someone to step up in a closing role.”

The roster is deep at the catching position with returner Hayden Stork and transfer Jack Snyder, along with freshmen Chris Suchoski and Caden Martin.

The infield will be anchored by returning players Danny Puplava (1st base) and Andy Onnen (3rd base), who are both coming off outstanding freshman seasons. They are joined by transfer Trevor Walters and freshmen Gavin Noreus, Tyler Thompson, Connor Janik, and Gavin DeBerry

The outfield consists of returners Poco Paulina—who has transitioned from short stop to center field—and Braedon MacDonald (left field), along with freshmen Curtis McKay, Cole Freeman, and Jairus Harris.

“We are excited about our team and looking forward to getting this season started.” said Post. “Timely hitting, consistent pitching, and good defense are always the keys to winning in a very competitive Region 4 Tournament in May.”
