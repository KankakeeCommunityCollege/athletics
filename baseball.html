 Season Preview

The Cavaliers enter the 2026 season with a chip on their shoulders after a memorable 2025 campaign that ended just short of an at-large national tournament bid. After finishing 46-10, and climbing to No. 8 in the country, going to Enid is still the standard and the target. The Cavs are more motivated than ever to earn their way back to the national tournament.

Repeating last year’s pitching success won’t be easy after losing two of the most reliable arms in the country. Peyton Niksch, now signed with the Milwaukee Brewers, and Cole Martz (St. Xavier University) leave behind sub-2.00 ERAs and a massive footprint on the staff.

Head Coach Bryce Shafer believes this group can be special, just in a different way.

Three starters have proven big inning experience. Clay Gadbois, Kollin Asbury and Maddox Quesnelle are all ready to anchor the early season. Joe Catalano is shifting from the closer role into the rotation, where he’s looking to lead with both his stuff and his presence. KCC will have plenty of depth from spot starters and a high-leverage bullpen. Two-way threats Lucas Rink and Hank Vandermeer give the staff even more flexibility, capable of providing starts or late-inning outs when the moment demands it.

Dominic Panozzo, Caleb Clutteur and Chase Harrison are all pushing for major roles to finish games, while sophomores Trevor Syer and Zach Wilson return after valuable bullpen innings in 2025. Carter Ponton, Luke Gallet and Ben Kiracofe are also competing for key spots. Easton Blankenship and Caden Keleminic offer additional options as starters or impactful weapons out of the pen. Relievers Trey Harney and Alex Bielfeldt also add depth, giving the Cavs even more flexibility as roles take shape.

Offensively, six starters graduated, and a revamped lineup has opportunities everywhere. The transfer sophomores who are ready to make noise immediately are catcher Connor Gaitens, first baseman Hank Vandermeer, and catcher Levi Hirst. They all have power bats expected to impact the lineup from day one.

Returning with starting experience, utility Thomas Offill, third baseman Jack Vogt, and first baseman Trey Blanchette provide needed stability to a group that will feature plenty of new faces. Charles Cambridge adds another option at third base, while Easton Urban brings depth and versatility as an infielder.

A talented freshman class will be counted on to grow up fast. Infielders Layson Fabisiak, Drew Stabler, Lucas Rink, and Gabe McHugh are all competing for innings. The outfield competition is just as wide open, with Josh Davis, Matt Swartz, Deegan Aeilts, Layson Fabisiak, JJ Gillespie, and Lucas Rink provide flexibility and athleticism. Angel Nelson also gives the Cavs another outfield option as the lineup takes shape.

“This is the most competitive offseason I have been a part of at KCC,” Coach Shafer said. “There are a lot of unknowns and innings up for grabs in the lineup this spring. I am excited to watch the guys continue to compete and see who rises to the top. We have the deepest pitching staff that we’ve had in a while but will need inexperienced guys to step up and throw impactful innings for us.”

While the lineup may feature more power than in past seasons, the Cavaliers remain rooted in a pitching-and-defense-first philosophy. That identity will guide the early season as the new pieces settle in. With disappointment from 2025 still driving daily urgency, the mission is clear: keep developing, keep competing, and earn a postseason opportunity.

The Cavaliers are excited to get started. 2026 is set up to be a fast, competitive, and hungry season for the Cavs.
