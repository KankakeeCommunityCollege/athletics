 Season Preview

A new chapter begins for the Lady Cavs for 2025, as the program welcomes Head Coach Jill Morgan and Assistant Coach Marty Walters. With a mix of returning experience and promising new talent, the Cavaliers are looking to continue their momentum in the competitive Region 4.

“I'm excited to build something special here,” said Coach Morgan. “The foundation is in place with our returners, and we have added some hungry, talented new comers who are ready to step in and compete."

The team brings back six players from last season, including red shirt Emily Cockream, providing a solid foundation for the incoming class. Leading the charge are sophomores Kennedi Huston, a standout OH known for her vocal leadership, all-around play and ability at any position; and Libby Green, from Kankakee, a right side hitter with a powerful presence at the net and the ability to cover all aspects of the court. Joining them is Martina Rivolta, a dynamic setter from Italy, who's expected to be a key piece in the team's evolving offense. Also returning is our libero, Andrijana Cicvaric from Serbia, who will step back into her role of reading opposing offenses. Rounding out our talented returners is MB Alyssa Johnston, a pivotal player with ability to find the open spot.

Together, the returning players offer continuity, chemistry, and a sense of stability as the program transitions under new leadership.

Coach Morgan brought in an exciting class of newcomers, who bring athleticism, versatility, and a hunger to prove themselves.

Janice Jackson a 5'11" MB from Kankakee is impressive with her command at the net and power in her serve. Local talents, and twin sisters, Ana and Alyssa Pignatiello look to make their presence known in Region 4 play, providing even more power and options at the net! Kendyl Christon is also a local who will bring versatility at all positions. Defensive specialists Maddie Casino and Nature Shelburn will provide court awareness and grit to the Cavs defense.

“This group has come in ready to work! Coach Morgan said. "They are learning quickly and adapting to this new level of play. They are pushing our returners in the gym and it will be a fun year watching them work together on the court.”

The Cavs open up their season at the OWT tournament in Rockford facing some tough competition before Region 4 play begins. It will be a crucial early test for a team still learning each other's rhythms. With region play right around the corner, Coach Morgan is focused on building a gritty, accountable, and relentless team.

“This is about building a foundation," Coach Morgan said. "We want to be the team everyone talks about: competitive on every point, tough defensively, scrappy, together in every huddle and playing with a passion to show that Lady Cavaliers volleyball is on the rise."
